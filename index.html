Scheduled Utilities Forecast - SHEPD (North) - Tuesday 30 Mar 2021

Day 1 of 5 (0600 Wednesday 17th February to 0600 Thursday 18th February)

 

General Forecast - Perth, Angus, Aberdeen, Moray Firth
Another front and associated area of low pressure moves through during the morning lingering into the afternoon. Light to moderate showers are possible from this passing front, with scattered showers lingering through the afternoon. Overnight, another low pressure system nears the country, bringing further showers to western coastlines. No hazards expected, though rather breezy with gusts approaching 55mph acorss exposed parts of Moray Firth and Perth and Angus.
General Forecast - Western Isles, NW & Cent Highlands, Skye, Argyll
Another front will traverse the region in the morning lingering into the afternoon where light to moderate showers are possible, especially near the coasts. Any snow that will fall will remain light and above 200m. Wet conditions will continue overnight, with gusty winds lingering across the western and northern coasts, highest in the Western Isles, where gusts could reach 70mph this evning. A stronger area of low pressure nears the western coast into the late overnight with additional light rain/snowfall possible, though any snowfall below 200m will be trace as warmer conditions persist. Lightning risk is low. 
General Forecast - Shetland, Orkney, NE Caithness
Dry during the morning ahead of another front which will give way to scattered light showers into the afternoon. There is a low risk of wet snow mixing above 200m but should not lead to accumulations. Dry conditions prevail into the evening and overnight. No hazards expected.
Synoptic Discussion and Confidence
A passing front in the morning will bring associated precipitation to the area, with a chance for snowfall to mix in above 200m. Winds will remain gusty across northern and western coastlines as the front passes. Another low will approach the region late during the overnight hours, bringing further precipitation to the area.
Upland Forecast
Some sleet or wet snow is possible at higher elevations, otherwise no signinficant differences.

Scheduled Utilities Forecast - SHEPD (North) - Tuesday 30 Mar 2021

Day 2 of 5 (0600 Thursday 18th February to 0600 Friday 19th February)

 

General Forecast - Perth, Angus, Aberdeen, Moray Firth
Another area of low pressure will move through N Scotland, bringing a cold front across the territory. This will bring spells of moderate to heavy showers across the all regions during the morning and early afternoon, with a low risk of lightning near the coast. Low risk of snow mixing above 200m. Showers will continue throughout the day, slowly tapering after the low moves northeast of the region in the afternoon, and slowly taper off through the evening and overnight. No hazards expected.
General Forecast - Western Isles, NW & Cent Highlands, Skye, Argyll
Another low pressure system will cross through the territory near dawn on Thursday, and continue through the afternoon before passing to the north. Showers will continue throughout the day, tapering to scattered as the system passes. A chance for some further, elevated snow above 200m remains possible, but anything below 200m will be trace amounts. Winds will remain gusty throughout the day, tapering through the day, and increasing near 0600 on Friday as yet another frontal boundary approaches the territory. No hazards are expected.
General Forecast - Shetland, Orkney, NE Caithness
Another front will cross the region late in the morning and early afternoon on Thursday. This will bring light to moderate precipitation during the morning, before rainfall slowly taper throughout the afternoon and evening as the front and associated low pressure move to the North. No hazards are expected.
Synoptic Discussion and Confidence
A passing frontal boundary and the associated low pressure system will bring further precipitation across the island, with near hazard gusts early in the period. Another approaching system will causes wind gusts and precipitation to increase near the end of the time period as well.
Upland Forecast
Some light upland snow above 200m is possible, with a few centimetres of snow possible.

General Forecast - Perth, Angus, Aberdeen, Moray Firth
Another front will traverse the territory early on Friday. This will bring widespread showers throughout the morning, before the front moves to the east by the afternoon. General Forecast - Western Isles, NW & Cent Highlands, Skye, Argyll
